## AuroraDB's Research-Driven Innovation

### Understanding Database Trade-offs
Database migrations occur because each database makes different trade-offs. AuroraDB's innovation lies in **research-backed approaches to minimize these trade-offs**:

### PostgreSQL/MySQL → ClickHouse: Analytics Performance vs. ACID
**The Problem:** Traditional RDBMS struggle with analytical workloads
**Research Opportunity:** Can columnar storage, vectorization, and compression be combined with ACID?

### PostgreSQL/MySQL → Cassandra: Scale vs. Consistency
**The Problem:** RDBMS scaling limitations and eventual consistency challenges
**Research Opportunity:** Can strong consistency scale through advanced protocols and architectures?

### PostgreSQL/MySQL → TiDB: HTAP vs. Performance
**The Problem:** Distributed systems often sacrifice single-node performance
**Research Opportunity:** Can distributed databases achieve both scale and speed?

### The AuroraDB Hypothesis
**AuroraDB demonstrates that research breakthroughs can significantly reduce—but not eliminate—these trade-offs.**
